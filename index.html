Welcome to Mark Decker's homepage!

I developed this website to document and share my research and teaching. I'm an academic research scientist specializing in terrestrial and land-atmosphere processes in weather and climate. I am most proud of my continuing model development research; contributing to the weather and climate models that forecast weather tomorrow and indicate future climates.

In the long term I plan to add earth science, climate, weather, terrestrial, and hydrological information and resources to this site; spreading knowledge and sharing my passions.

My Story

Growing up in Colorado I spent many night backpacking and days fishing; likely why I love nature. In contrast to many I also demonstrated a nearly uncontrollable desire to know how everthing works. As a toddler I took apart remote controls and other devices. My parents saved out possesions by giving me chemistry sets, electronics sets, a microscope, and eventuallly letting my 9 year old self build a lawn mower engine powered go kart by myself. Eventually I discovered that math and physics not only explain dynamical and mechanistic processes but are also capabale of predicting future events! My research is a perfect asnwer to passions that began as a child; my need for discovery, and the beauty of math and physics.

I never envisioned that I would follow in the foot steps of my Mom and Sister to teach; I discovered teaching to be as rewardful as research during University. Although my Brother is working to become a teacher, so nature and nuture may have predestined my teaching passion.

I've lived in Australia the past seven years after finishing my PhD, partly to experience to world and partly because the ecology is unique, but primarily to work with internationally renowned scienmtists. I now have the research and teaching experience, expertise, and track record to become an assistant professor in the US. I am currently applying to positions in departments with complementary expertise; ensuring the postion benefits myself and the department.

Why a custom webpage?

I wanted to create a long lived vehicle to share my published, in progress, and future research, my teaching philosophy and experience, and tidbits about myself. Google scholar and ORCID contain my publication record, however ideas on future research and teaching are nelgected. Ultimately I needed a single place where students, potential employers, and possible collaborators are provided with a record of my past, present, and future (well possible future) work.

Math, physics, and science have transformed society. Everything that now surrounds us (from cars to plastic spoons) was made possible because of the knowledge discovered and dissiminated by scientists. Science benefits society when society understands the scientific process and believes the results. Unfortunately it seems much of society (atleast in the US) do not believe of trust scientists. I want to build a platform to spread my passion for math and science, particularly for weather and climate. I feel a scientificly literate population would be gauranteed if everyone witnessed the beauty I see in predicting the future using physcs and math. The design and creation of material that spreads my scientific passion is under development, so come back soon!

I have an innate desire to know the mechanisms and processes in the world around me. I took apart remote controls and watches as a toddler and discovering and explaining hydrometeorological processes is my career (and passion). However my curiousity is unlimited, and I had to learn how webpages are built in html and css. Originally my page used liteaccordion, but has now moved to w3.css.
