Welcome to my website

My name is Mark Decker and I am currently a research fellow at the Centre of Excellence for Climate System Science at the Univserity of New south Wales.

My approach to scientific discovery utilizes my knowledge of physical processes, mathematical techniques, and observations to develop, improve, and utilize climate and weather models. My specific focus has been on land and land-atmosphere interactions at scales from a point to the globe. My research stands at the nexus of atmospheric science, climate science, hydrology, and ecology, with implications for water management, agriculture, and climate and weather prediction.

I have contributed to the model development of the land surface models in the Community Earth System Model (CESM) and the Australian Community Climate Eaarth System Simulator (ACCESS)


Research Interests

  • Model Development and Evaluation
  • Hydrology in large scale models
  • Turbulent fluxes
  • Land-atmosphere coupling
  • Anthropogenic activities such as agriculture
  • Climate and numerical weather prediction
