Why Teach?

My desire to and passion for teaching is selfish and altruistic

  • Teaching is a form of instant (or near term) gratification. My personal satisfaction from enabling an 'oh now I get it moment', or accomplishments once though impossible, or sparking interest in math or science. Even the extra time teaching provides to talk about my passions is rewardful. Only recently this satisfaction waw surpassed by similar moments with my daughter.
  • I firmly believe that a general population educated in math, science (e.g. earth science), and the scientific method is a vital part of a functioning democracy. Through voting, people express their desires related to environmental policy decisions. An uneducated society is unable to make informed decisions on which canditates will pursue evidence based policy choices. If a majority of people believed mecrury safe and capable of transforming anyone into a super hero like the X-Man Wolverine (it is extremely dangerous! Do not try!) elected officials wouldn't waste resources preventing mercury exposre. Mercury would poison everyone!

My philosophy and Style

Learning varies with individuals; no one method provides a universally ideal approach. However any technique that generates student interest and maximizes student engagement is always beneficial. My methods for maximizing interest and engagement include the following.

  • Purposely inhibiting my instincts to hide my passion for everything earth science
  • Providing metaphors and analogies using real world items and topics important to students when explaining complex ideas
  • Minimize any actual lecturing. Instead I use questions and guide answers. Yes I expect everyone to participate!
  • Solve problems in class using small groups, selected to ensure every group has a broad range of backgrounds and opinions.

Why a custom webpage?

I wanted to create a long lived vehicle to share my published, in progress, and future research, my teaching philosophy and experience, and tidbits about myself. Google scholar and ORCID contain my publication record, however ideas on future research and teaching are nelgected. Ultimately I needed a single place where students, potential employers, and possible collaborators are provided with a record of my past, present, and future (well possible future) work.

Math, physics, and science have transformed society. Everything that now surrounds us (from cars to plastic spoons) was made possible because of the knowledge discovered and dissiminated by scientists. Science benefits society when society understands the scientific process and believes the results. Unfortunately it seems much of society (atleast in the US) do not believe of trust scientists. I want to build a platform to spread my passion for math and science, particularly for weather and climate. I feel a scientificly literate population would be gauranteed if everyone witnessed the beauty I see in predicting the future using physcs and math. The design and creation of material that spreads my scientific passion is under development, so come back soon!
